# The Sentence

The group of words like this, which makes a complete sense is called a Sentence

A **sentence** is a group of words that expresses a complete thought.

### Example:
- I am learning English.
- She likes reading books.

## Kind of Sentences 

### 1. Asserative or Declarative
Those which make a statements or assertions as

- Humpty Dumpty sat on a wall.

### 2. Interrogative
Those which ask a question; as

- Where do you live?
- What is your name? 
- How old are you?

### 3. Imperative
Those which express commands, requests, or entreaties; as,

- Be quiet.
- Have mercy upon us.

### 4. Exclamatory
Those which express strong feelings; as,

- How cold the night is!
- What a shame!


# Subject and Predicate

We name some person or thing; and
Say something about that person or thing.
In other words, we must have a *subject* to speak about and we must say or predicate something about the subject.
### Hence every sentence has two parts: 

## Subject
- The part which names the person or thing we are speaking about. This is called the **Subject** of the sentence.
## Predicate
- The part which tells something about the *Subject*. This is called the **Predicate** of the sentence.

## Rules Common: 

### 1. Subject usually comes first 
The Subject of a sentence usually comes first, but occasionally it is put after the Predicate; as. 
    - Here comes the bus.
    - Sweet are the uses of adversity.

### Normal order of a sentence:
Subject + Verb + Object

### Example:
- I am learning English.
- She likes reading.

### Here:
- Subject = who does the action
- Verb = the action

### 2. Imperative Sentences  
In imperative sentences, the **subject is not written**, but it is **understood**. 
- Sit down. [Here the subject *You* is understood.]
- Thank him. [Here too the Subject *You* is understood.]

| Sentence Type        | Subject Description        |
|----------------------|----------------------------|
| Normal Sentence      | Subject is clearly stated  |
| Imperative Sentence  | Subject is hidden (you)    |

### Summary : 
In most sentences, the subject comes first, but in commands (imperative sentences), the subject â€œyouâ€ is understood and not written.
